On 2022-11-20


Developers from Schaffer-Nishimura Lab have created a miniature VR system, the MouseVRheadset. This small virtual reality headset was designed to study the behavior of mice in a variety of virtual environments. Indeed, the subjects are immersed in interactive and non-interactive visual scenes.

These behavioral mouse experiments are intended to advance neuroscience research and the monocular display is ideal for simple visual stimulation for basic visual neuroscience applications.

How does it work?


The helmet is 3D printed and incorporates all the necessary components including two small screens at a resolution of 240 x 210 allowing simple virtual reality experiences.

A spherical treadmill was designed in order to collect and use mouse movement information, as well as to allow them to navigate freely.

Optical sensors and a microcontroller allow the measurement of other data such as when the mice move on the treadmill, the optical sensors follow these movements and translate the data into a 2D movement. The game engine then takes care of translating these movements into appropriate camera movements in the virtual scene.


Instructions on building the MouseVRheadset can be found on GitHub. It is therefore possible to conduct your own behavioral research using personalized VR technology. A few specific parts are required as well as access to a 3D printer. Developers provide links to all necessary software. This includes the Raspberry Pi operating system and the Godot game engine.
